Highlights of this Course

EE 504 introduces the methods of filtering for stationary and non-stationary signals. The discussed topics include linear estimation problems, wiener filtering for stationary signals and adaptive filters and Kalman Filters for non-stationary signals with unknown or partially known charachteristics. The course presents many application example such as the channel identification and equalization (for modem communications),  noise-echo cancellation, speech coding (linear predictive coding) etc. 

Course Description

Some familarity with the random processes and linear algebra is assumed. The prior courses of EE 501 (Linear Sytem Theory) and EE 503 (Random Processes) is highly recommended. Students are encouraged to take these pre-requisted and make themselves comfortable with their contents to make best of the offered course.
